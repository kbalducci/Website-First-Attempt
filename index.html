Gluten, We Have a Problem.

Gluten is a common buzzword. It is popping up in the news, on food labels, in magazines, on the cover of best-selling books, at your child's school - seemingly everywhere except your Doctor's Office. So, how do you know if you have a problem with gluten?

The laundry list of possible gluten-related illnesses include:

  • IBS
  • Eczema
  • Any Autoimmune Disease
  • Hypothyroidism
  • Asthma
  • Osteoporosis
  • Chronic Fatigue Syndrome
  • ADD/ADHD
  • Bipolar
  • Migraines or unexplained headaches
  • Infertility or recurrent miscarriage

If any of these sound like you, gluten might be your problem.

To determine if gluten is not your friend you can get tested via blood, stool, urine or cheek swab or do an elimination diet. Okay, let's talk testing - if you bring this up with your primary care doctor they will probably give you a blood test for celiac antibodies. This could be helpful, if you in fact have celiac disease. However, many people with gluten issues do not have celiac. They might have non-celiac gluten sensitivity or intolerance.

So, what other tests are there? When I was diagnosed with gluten intolerance, I was seeing an Integrative Medicine Doctor who ordered Diagnos-Techs Expanded GI and Saliva panel. It did show that I had Gliadin Antibodies and therefore was intolerant to gluten. However, it is now known that people who react to gluten could react to various proteins, not just gliadin. I happened to react to gliadin so the test was positive. However, if I reacted to a different gluten protein the test would have been a false negative. If I were just starting out, I would recommend a more comprehensive test that tests for reactions to more antibodies or genetic testing.
